Serbian Republic

Serbia (/ˈsɜːrbiə/ (audio speaker iconlisten), SUR-bee-ə; Serbian: Србија, Srbija, pronounced [sř̩bija] (audio speaker iconlisten)), officially the Republic of Serbia (Serbian: Република Србија, Republika Srbija, pronounced [repǔblika sř̩bija] (audio speaker iconlisten)), is a landlocked country in Southeast Europe, at the crossroads of the Pannonian Plain and the Balkans. It shares land borders with Hungary to the north, Romania to the northeast, Bulgaria to the southeast, North Macedonia to the south, Croatia and Bosnia and Herzegovina to the west, and Montenegro to the southwest, and claiming a border with Albania through the disputed territory of Kosovo.[a] Serbia has a population of roughly 7 million inhabitants. Its capital Belgrade is also the largest city.
